Genetic Social Psychology Lab and UCFS

Research tools for social representations, intergroup relations, and peacebuilding.

Grounded in Genetic Social Psychology, this platform explores how social representations are constructed, contested, and transformed through interaction. It brings together digital tools for examining public understandings of the Cyprus problem, identifying possible common ground, and supporting evidence-informed dialogue across communities.

Research Tools

Interactive tools and dashboards

These modules can begin as linked apps and later become part of a shared dashboard with common navigation, data access, and user settings.

Pilot

Common Ground Search Tool / “Habermas Machine”

A pilot tool that helps groups of citizens identify points of convergence, even when they begin from different or opposing positions. Users can express views and the tool attempts to produce a synthetic formulation of possible common ground.

Open tool
AI tool

Cyprus Solution Landscape AI

A documentation tool that allows natural-language questions and synthetic answers based on existing research knowledge about the Cyprus problem: attitudes, fears, hopes, red lines, acceptance of solution forms, and possible zones of agreement.

Open tool
Research app

Cyprus Issue Representations App

A tool focused on the social representations of the Cyprus problem: how people give meaning to the solution, the other community, security, justice, and the future, beyond simple support or rejection of an idea.

Open tool
Prediction tool

Cyprus Conjoint Predictions

An interactive prediction tool for exploring how different combinations of provisions in a possible Cyprus settlement may shape public support.
